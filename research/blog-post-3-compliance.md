# Epistemic AI for Compliance: Meeting Regulatory Standards

## The Compliance Challenge

As AI systems increasingly handle critical business decisions—from credit approvals to medical diagnoses—regulators are paying attention. The EU AI Act, NIST AI Risk Management Framework, and SOC 2 requirements now explicitly address AI transparency and reliability. Organizations face a pressing question: **How do we prove our AI is trustworthy enough for regulated use?**

## The Regulatory Landscape

### EU AI Act
The European Union's comprehensive AI regulation emphasizes transparency obligations for high-risk AI systems. Deployers must understand AI capabilities and limitations—exactly what epistemic integrity provides.

### NIST AI RMF
The U.S. National Institute of Standards and Technology framework calls for measuring AI reliability and managing AI-specific risks, including hallucination detection and confidence calibration.

### SOC 2
Service organization controls now routinely evaluate AI reliability. Auditors look for evidence that AI systems produce consistent, verifiable outputs.

## How Abraxas Addresses Compliance

Epistemic integrity—ensuring AI outputs accurately represent what the model knows and doesn't know—directly maps to regulatory requirements:

### 1. Uncertainty Quantification
When AI explicitly labels uncertain responses ([UNCERTAIN], [LOW CONFIDENCE]), organizations can implement appropriate review workflows for high-risk outputs.

### 2. Calibration Verification
Abraxas tests whether model confidence matches actual accuracy. A well-calibrated model means audit trails can rely on confidence scores as risk indicators.

### 3. Transparency Labels
Explicit epistemic markers create auditable trails:
- [CONFIDENT] - High accuracy expected
- [UNCERTAIN] - Requires human review
- [UNKNOWN] - Model lacks relevant knowledge

## Compliance Checklist

Use this checklist to evaluate your AI compliance posture:

- [ ] **Risk Assessment** - Have you identified all AI use cases requiring oversight?
- [ ] **Confidence Calibration** - Does your model accurately represent its certainty?
