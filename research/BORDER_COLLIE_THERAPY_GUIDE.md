## Executive Summary

Training a Border Collie as a therapy dog is an excellent choice—their intelligence, trainability, and sensitivity make them outstanding candidates. However, their high energy and herding instincts require specific management. This guide provides a complete roadmap from basic obedience through certification, with Davis County-specific resources.

**Timeline:** 12-18 months from puppy to certified therapy team  
**Estimated Cost:** $800-2,500 (training, testing, insurance, equipment)  
**Key Commitment:** Consistent weekly training + socialization

## 1. Therapy Dog Certification Organizations

### Major Organizations Comparison

| Organization | Testing Requirements | Insurance Included | Renewal | Annual Fee | Notes |
|-------------|---------------------|-------------------|---------|------------|-------|
| **Alliance of Therapy Dogs (ATD)** | Skills test + 3-4 observed visits | Yes | Annual | ~$50-65 | Most popular, strong support network |
| **Pet Partners** | Skills test + evaluation team training | Yes | 2 years | ~$70-95 | Rigorous, highly respected |
| **Therapy Dogs International (TDI)** | AKC Canine Good Citizen + TDI test | Yes | Annual | ~$40-50 | Requires CGC first |
| **Love on a Leash** | LOAL evaluation test | Yes | Annual | ~$50 | Growing organization |

### Alliance of Therapy Dogs (ATD) - Recommended for Border Collies

**Why ATD:**
- Less rigid than Pet Partners,更适合 high-energy breeds
- Strong insurance coverage ($1M liability)
- Excellent mentorship through Tester/Observer program
- Flexible visit scheduling

**5-Step Certification Process:**

1. **Background Check** - Required for all handlers ($25-30 fee)
2. **Download & Complete Paperwork** - Membership application, veterinary records
3. **Find a Volunteer Tester/Observer** - ATD provides local contacts
4. **Skills Assessment + 3-4 Supervised Observations** - Handler + dog evaluated
5. **Submit Paperwork + Fees** - Begin visiting immediately after approval

**Testing Standards:**
- Dog must be minimum 1 year old
- Up-to-date vaccinations (rabies, DHPP, bordetella)
- Negative fecal exam within 12 months
- Clean bill of health from veterinarian

### Pet Partners

**Stricter Requirements:**
- Mandatory handler education course (online + test)
- More rigorous skills evaluation
- 2-year certification (vs. annual for others)
- Excellent for hospital/clinical settings

### TDI (Therapy Dogs International)

**Prerequisites:**
- AKC Canine Good Citizen (CGC) required first
- TDI skills test follows CGC
- Good stepping stone for new handlers

## 2. Required Commands & Skills Checklist

### Essential Obedience Commands (Non-Negotiable)

| Command | Description | Therapy Context |
|---------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Sit** | Immediate sit on verbal/hand signal | Greeting patients, waiting |
| **Down** | Immediate down stay (3+ minutes) | Calm presence in rooms |
| **Stay** | Hold position until released | Safety around medical equipment |
| **Come** | Reliable recall (distractions) | Emergency situations |
| **Heel** | Loose-leash walking | Hospital corridors, crowded areas |
| **Leave It** | Ignore food/toys/interests | Patient food, dropped items |
| **Drop It** | Release items from mouth | Safety with patient belongings |
| **Off** | All four paws on floor | No jumping on patients |

### Advanced Therapy Skills

| Skill | Description | Training Timeline |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Settle on Cue** | Calm down immediately | Month 6-8 |
| **Ignore Medical Equipment** | No reaction to IV poles, wheelchairs | Month 4-6 |
| **Treat Manners** | Gentle take, no snatching | Month 3-5 |
| **Greeting Behavior** | Calm approach, no crowding | Month 5-7 |
| **Surface Tolerance** | Walk on tile, carpet, grating | Month 3-4 |
| **Noise Tolerance** | Ignore alarms, beeps, cries | Month 4-6 |
| **Crowd Tolerance** | Navigate busy spaces calmly | Month 6-8 |
| **Wheelchair/Walker Acceptance** | No reaction to mobility aids | Month 4-5 |

### Border Collie-Specific Skills

| Challenge | Training Solution |
|-----------|-------------------|
| **Herding Instinct** | "Leave It" + redirection to incompatible behavior |
| **High Energy** | Pre-visit exercise, settle cue training |
| **Sensitivity** | Gradual exposure, positive counter-conditioning |
| **Eye Stare** | Teach soft eye contact, break stare on cue |
| **Barking Tendency** | "Quiet" command, threshold training |

## 3. Training Methodology & Timeline

### Positive Reinforcement Framework

**Core Principles:**
- Reward-based training (treats, praise, play)
- No punishment-based methods (therapy dogs must associate work positively)
- Short sessions (5-15 minutes, multiple daily)
- End on success

**Recommended Tools:**
- High-value treats (chicken, cheese, commercial training treats)
- Clicker for marking behavior
- Standard 6-foot leash (no retractable)
- Flat collar or front-clip harness (no prong/choke)

### Month-by-Month Training Progression

#### **Months 1-3: Foundation Obedience**

**Focus:**
- Sit, down, stay (1 minute)
- Come (low distraction)
- Loose-leash walking
- Name recognition
- Crate training (if applicable)

**Border Collie Notes:**
- Start mental stimulation early (puzzle toys, nose work)
- Watch for over-arousal; keep sessions calm
- Begin socialization immediately

**Milestones:**
- Reliable sit/down in quiet environment
- 30-second stay with handler 3 feet away
- Walk without pulling for 10 minutes

---

#### **Months 4-6: Intermediate Skills**

**Focus:**
- Stay (3 minutes, moderate distraction)
- Leave it (food, toys)
- Heel with turns/stops
- Surface variations
- Noise exposure (recordings, then real)

**Border Collie Notes:**
- Channel energy into structured activities
- Introduce "settle" cue after exercise
- Begin desensitization to medical sounds

**Milestones:**
- Pass AKC Canine Good Citizen test items
- Ignore dropped food on command
- Walk calmly past wheelchairs/walkers

---

#### **Months 7-9: Advanced Therapy Prep**

**Focus:**
- Extended stay (5+ minutes)
- Greeting protocol (approach, settle, disengage)
- Treat-taking manners
- Ignore medical equipment
- Crowd navigation

**Border Collie Notes:**
- Practice "off-switch" after high activity
- Work on soft eye contact (no intense staring)
- Introduce sudden movements without reaction

**Milestones:**
- 5-minute down stay with distractions
- Gentle treat acceptance from strangers
- Navigate busy corridor without pulling

---

#### **Months 10-12: Certification Prep**

**Focus:**
- Mock testing scenarios
- 3-4 observed visits (for ATD)
- Handler education (patient interaction, safety)
- Documentation completion

**Border Collie Notes:**
- Ensure dog can "power down" after visits
- Monitor for overstimulation signs
- Build visit stamina gradually

**Milestones:**
- Pass mock therapy dog test
- Complete required observations
- Submit all paperwork

---

#### **Months 13-18: Active Therapy Work**

**Focus:**
- Regular scheduled visits
- Ongoing skill maintenance
- Continuing education
- Renewal preparation

**Border Collie Notes:**
- Limit visits to prevent burnout (2-3/month initially)
- Watch for stress signals (panting, pacing, whale eye)
- Maintain exercise routine outside therapy work

## 5. Border Collie-Specific Training Tips

### Breed Strengths for Therapy Work

✅ **High Intelligence** - Learns commands quickly, retains training  
✅ **Eager to Please** - Motivated by handler praise  
✅ **Sensitive** - Reads human emotions well (therapeutic benefit)  
✅ **Athletic** - Can handle physical demands of visits  
✅ **Trainable** - Excels at complex skill chains  

### Breed Challenges to Address

⚠️ **High Energy** - Must be exercised BEFORE visits  
⚠️ **Herding Instinct** - May try to "herd" patients; redirect incompatible behavior  
⚠️ **Sensitivity** - Can be overwhelmed by chaotic environments  
⚠️ **Intense Eye Contact** - "The stare" can intimidate; teach soft eyes  
⚠️ **Barking Tendency** - Alert barking must be managed  
⚠️ **Mental Stimulation Needs** - Boredom leads to destructive behavior  

### Border Collie Therapy Training Protocol

**Pre-Visit Routine:**
1. 30-45 minutes vigorous exercise (run, fetch, agility)
2. 10 minutes settling/calming
3. Potty break
4. Calm departure (no excited energy)

**During Visit:**
- Watch for overstimulation (panting, pacing, pinned ears, whale eye)
- Take breaks every 20-30 minutes
- Use "settle" cue between interactions
- Keep visits under 60 minutes initially

**Post-Visit:**
- Decompression time (quiet space, water)
- No additional high-energy activity
- Monitor for stress signs (changes in appetite, sleep, behavior)

**Warning Signs of Burnout:**
- Reluctance to approach patients
- Increased startle response
- Excessive panting/drooling
- Avoidance behaviors
- Changes in bathroom habits

**If burnout occurs:**
- Reduce visit frequency
- Shorter visit duration
- More calming activities between visits
- Consider 2-4 week break if severe

## 6. Utah Requirements & Legal Considerations

### State Laws

**Utah Therapy Dog Laws:**
- No state-wide therapy dog registration required
- Therapy dogs are NOT service dogs (no public access rights)
- Access granted by facility permission only
- Handler must have organization certification + insurance

### Liability Insurance

**Coverage Through Organizations:**
- ATD: $1M liability coverage included in membership
- Pet Partners: $1M liability + excess medical
- TDI: Coverage included with membership

**Important:**
- Insurance ONLY covers approved therapy visits
- Does NOT cover personal activities
- Keep documentation of each visit (date, location, supervisor)

### Facility Access Rules

**Typical Requirements:**
- Current certification from recognized organization
- Up-to-date vaccination records
- Health certificate from veterinarian
- Facility-specific orientation
- Scheduled visits (no drop-in)
- Handler supervision at all times

**Restricted Areas:**
- ICU (varies by facility)
- Operating rooms
- Isolation rooms (immunocompromised patients)
- Psychiatric units (facility-dependent)

### Documentation Requirements

**Keep Records Of:**
- Each visit (date, time, location, duration)
- Patient interactions (general notes)
- Any incidents (bites, scratches, equipment issues)
- Continuing education credits
- Renewal dates

**Required Documents:**
- Organization certification card
- Liability insurance certificate
- Veterinary health records
- Handler background check (organization-specific)

## 12. Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Training Mistakes

❌ **Starting therapy training too early** (wait until 12+ months)  
❌ **Skipping CGC foundation** (builds essential skills)  
❌ **Over-training in single session** (Border Collies burn out)  
❌ **Ignoring herding instincts** (must redirect, not punish)  
❌ **Insufficient socialization** (critical for therapy work)  

### Certification Mistakes

❌ **Choosing wrong organization** (match to dog's temperament)  
❌ **Incomplete paperwork** (delays certification)  
❌ **Skipping observed visits** (required for ATD)  
❌ **Not reading insurance terms** (know coverage limits)  

### Visit Mistakes

❌ **Too many visits too soon** (build stamina gradually)  
❌ **Ignoring stress signals** (dog welfare first)  
❌ **Dropping in unannounced** (always schedule)  
❌ **Unsupervised dog** (handler must maintain control)  
❌ **Skipping post-visit decompression** (prevents burnout)
